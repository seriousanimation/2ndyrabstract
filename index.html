The Genesis of Abstraction

Abstract art did not appear overnight. It was a gradual shift away from realistic representation, evolving through various movements that challenged the very purpose of art. This timeline highlights the key milestones in that journey. Click on any point to learn more about a specific movement and its contribution.

The Artist's Approaches

How does an artist arrive at abstraction? There isn't a single path. The journey can begin from the visible world, from an internal sensation, or from the pure exploration of form itself. Select an approach below to see an explanation and a dynamic visual interpretation.

1. From Observation

Abstracting from a recognizable subject by stylizing its form or isolating its core elements.

2. From Sensation

Translating an internal or sensory experience, like music or emotion, into visual language.

3. From Scratch

Creating from pure intuition, focusing on the expressive qualities of color, line, and form.

Select an Approach

Choose one of the three methods above to explore the different pathways to creating abstract art. Each path offers a unique way of thinking and seeing.

Assignment Toolkit: The Concept Weaver

This tool is designed to help you brainstorm for your assignment. First, read the brief carefully. Then, use the selectors to weave together your chosen theme with the formal elements of art. Let this guide your creative exploration.

Assignment Brief

"You'll have to choose a set of three things of the same kind, like three times of day, three songs, three flavours, three emotions. You will then think of what scale, medium, type of mark making, colors, and composition would convey how you feel about each of them without showing their likeness or appearance."

Brainstorming Keywords:

Select a theme and a formal element to generate ideas. For example, how would the 'Color' for 'Joy' differ from the 'Color' for 'Sorrow'?
